Allocation Examples

Your points must sum to 100, and your choices should be logically consistent. Here are some examples.

Rationale

These are logical because they focus on related concepts.

Example A: Focuses on 'small' and 'tiny' as related concepts implied by 'little guy'.

Example B: A balanced view where 'little guy' could mean small, but also be an affectionate term for a normal or big dog.

Example C: Believes 'little guy' is most likely just a friendly term for a normal-sized dog.

These are illogical because they assign high probability to contradictory concepts.

Example D: Logically flawed. A dog cannot be both 'small' and 'huge' at the same time.

Example E: Logically flawed. 'Tiny' and 'huge' are mutually exclusive concepts.

Example F: Logically flawed. Gives equal weight to 'huge' and 'normal size', which are contradictory.
